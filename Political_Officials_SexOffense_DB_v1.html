Database Summary

Methodology & Editorial Standards

Purpose

This database documents political officials, candidates, party officials, and staffers of all parties who have been charged with, convicted of, or (in notable cases) credibly alleged to have committed sex offenses. It is a research tool, not advocacy. Both parties are covered equally. All entries require primary source verification.

This is not a partisan project. The intent is accurate documentation. A record with "No charge filed" does not belong in the same analysis as a conviction. Status precision is non-negotiable.

Inclusion Criteria

  • Held elected office, ran as a candidate, held an appointed party position, or was a paid/official party staffer
  • Was charged with, convicted of, or pled guilty to a sex offense — OR made credible public self-admission (flagged as "No charge filed")
  • Party affiliation verifiable through a primary source

Status Field Definitions

  • Convicted: Jury or bench verdict of guilty.
  • Pled Guilty: Guilty or no-contest plea entered. Note if to lesser charge.
  • Charged: Arrested or indicted. Case not yet resolved. Not a conviction.
  • Dismissed / Acquitted: Included so database is complete and not just a shame list.
  • No charge filed: Allegation or self-admission without criminal charge — clearly flagged.
  • Conviction Vacated: If conviction is overturned, record is updated — never deleted.

Party Affiliation Verification Hierarchy

  • 1. Official party ballot designation (Secretary of State candidate filing)
  • 2. Ballotpedia party listing with source citation
  • 3. FEC or FollowTheMoney campaign finance records
  • 4. Voter registration records (where public)
  • 5. Multiple independent news sources consistently reporting same party

Priority State Order

States were researched in order of NIBRS-reported per-capita combined sex offense rate (per the NIBRS Per Capita Report v2, 2019–2024 pooled): MT (152.34), UT (151.75), ND (142.68), NH (120.40), SD (117.83), ID (116.12), MI (114.15), CO (110.96), OK (104.52), AR (99.79), plus federal-level cases across all states. Research continues in NIBRS rate order.

Key Sources

  • DOJ Office of Public Affairs press releases (justice.gov/opa)
  • PACER — federal court records
  • State court portals (NC, SD, ND, SC, etc.)
  • Ballotpedia misconduct tracking (2017–2026)
  • GovTrack Legislator Misconduct Database
  • Wikipedia: "American politicians convicted of sex offences" category
  • AP statehouse reporting; PBS statehouse misconduct database
  • State-specific investigative outlets (NC: Asheville Watchdog, NC Newsline; ND: ND Monitor; SC: SC Daily Gazette)

Data Limitations

  • This database captures what was reported — it is not exhaustive
  • Local officials (city council, school board) are underrepresented in search coverage vs. state/federal officials
  • Historical cases pre-2000 have lower coverage due to limited digitized records
  • Some cases that surfaced in searches could not be verified to inclusion standard and were excluded
